About Me

I am a translational scientist with 5+ years of experience bridging discovery research and therapeutic development. My expertise spans metabolic disease modeling, iPSC-derived cell systems, and in vivo pharmacology, skill sets directly applicable to drug discovery, target validation, and preclinical development in biopharma.

I have worked across the full research lifecycle: from identifying disease mechanisms (Type 2 Diabetes beta-cell dysfunction, pancreatic cancer tumor microenvironment) to building high-throughput analysis pipelines that accelerate decision-making. At Gladstone Institutes, I develop iPSC-based disease models for neurodegeneration. Previously at UC San Diego, I characterized metabolic heterogeneity using advanced imaging and built automated MATLAB/ImageJ pipelines that improved analysis accuracy by 80%.

What sets me apart is my dual background in hands-on bench science and cross-functional project management. At Krishagni Solutions, I deployed lab informatics systems for 30+ global research institutions, managing regulatory compliance, workflow automation, and stakeholder alignment, skills critical for navigating matrixed biopharma environments.

What makes me unique is my ability to speak both languages: rigorous experimental science and scalable, product-driven strategy. At Krishagni Solutions, I deployed research informatics platforms for institutions across three continents, aligning clinical workflows, biospecimen automation, and compliance with international standards.

I thrive in fast-paced, collaborative settings where scientific rigor meets business impact. Whether it's optimizing assay development, troubleshooting complex workflows, or learning new platform technologies, I adapt quickly and deliver results.

I am seeking Research Associate, Scientist I/II, or Associate Scientist roles in biopharma/biotech where I can contribute to drug discovery, target validation, cellular therapeutics, or platform technology development across oncology, metabolic disease, immunology, or neuroscience.
