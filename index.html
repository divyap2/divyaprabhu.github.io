About Me

I am a bioengineer and translational researcher passionate about bridging discovery with impact, where imaging, cell biology, and engineered solutions converge to drive better diagnostics and therapies. My path into research wasn’t just academic—it was deeply personal. Watching loved ones face chronic diseases grounded my work in a sense of purpose that continues to guide me in every experiment, model, and dataset I work with.

At UC San Diego’s Kravets Lab, my thesis research focuses on calcium signaling and beta-cell network dynamics in Type 2 Diabetes. I investigate how intra-islet communication affects insulin secretion and metabolic coordination—work aimed at uncovering early biomarkers and therapeutic targets.

I’ve worked on pancreatic cancer in the Cheresh Lab, product-driven clinical research software at Krishagni Solutions, and thrive in collaborative environments where precision meets purpose.
